## The pitch in one line

Your dead {weeknight} becomes your best night of the month — we build and run it
with you, at founding terms, because we only take three houses.

## Structure of the conversation

### 1. Their room, not our product

Open on their slowest night and what it costs them. Numbers they said in the
interview, played back.

### 2. The founding-house offer

- One owned, venue-branded recurring night. We design the format with them.
- We run the first three together: promo kit, run-of-show, door list.
- Founding rate locked 12 months. Three slots, 90-day window, then the door closes.

### 3. The honest caveat

This is early. That is why it is cheap and why they get a direct line to the
founder. Founding houses shape what this becomes.

## Objection notes

- **"We tried event nights."** — Trivia rented them a crowd. This night is theirs:
  their brand, their list, their repeat attendance.
- **"Why only three?"** — One founder, serviced properly. Scarcity here is honest.
