## Rules of engagement

- DM the **person**, not the venue account, whenever findable.
- One clear ask per message. Never pitch the product in touch one.
- Follow up exactly once, 3–4 days later. Silence after that = `passed`, move on.
- Log every send in the pipeline the same day — the list is the company.

## First touch — brewery

> Hey {name} — I was in {venue} on {night} and the room was great, but I noticed
> {weeknight} looks quiet. I'm working with a few local spots on an owned
> recurring night (not trivia). Worth 15 minutes to hear how {venue} runs slow
> nights today? Not selling anything on the call.

### Why this works

Names a specific observation, asks for an interview not a sale, and pre-handles
the "another events guy" objection.

## First touch — sneaker / retail

> {name} — {venue}'s release nights clearly pull a crowd. Curious what happens
> the other 28 days. I'm researching how shops turn drop-crowds into regulars —
> 15 minutes this week?

## First touch — gym / climbing

> Hey {name} — regulars at {venue} basically live there, which most businesses
> would kill for. I'm studying how gyms turn that into community nights that
> bring in new members. Could I pick your brain for 15 minutes?

## Follow-up (all segments)

> Hey {name} — floating this back up. 15 minutes, I bring questions not a pitch.
> If it's a no, tell me and I'll stop bugging you.

## After the interview

Move stage to `interview` in the pipeline, write raw notes into
`research`, and only then decide whether the founding-house pitch goes out.
